# Elicitation Question Bank

Reusable interview questions, grouped by goal. Pick a handful per session (a good hour covers 8–12 questions with follow-ups) and copy them into a session plan (`TEMPLATE-session-plan.md`). Improve this bank whenever a question earns or wastes its airtime.

**Technique reminders:** ask "walk me through the last time…" rather than "how do you usually…" (specifics beat generalisations); follow every workaround with "what would happen if you couldn't do that?"; silence after an answer often buys the real answer.

## Process Discovery

- Walk me through the last time you did <task>, step by step — including the messy parts.
- Who touches this before it reaches you? Who takes it after you're done?
- What has to be true before you can start? What tells you you're finished?
- Which steps happen outside any system — email, spreadsheets, phone calls, memory?
- What's different at month-end / year-end / peak season?

## Pain Points & Workarounds

- Where does this go wrong most often? What does the failure look like?
- What do you do when the normal path is blocked? (Workarounds are requirements in disguise.)
- What do you keep your own copy/spreadsheet/notes of, and why don't you trust the system for it?
- If you could delete one step tomorrow, which one — and what would break?
- What do new team members get wrong until someone corrects them?

## Data & Integrations

- Where does the data you rely on come from, and how do you know when it's stale or wrong?
- What do you re-type from one system into another?
- Who else consumes what you produce, and in what format?
- What would you need to see on one screen to avoid switching systems?

## NFR Probes

- **Volume:** How many <transactions> on a normal day? On the worst day you can remember?
- **Latency:** When you submit <action>, how long can you wait before it disrupts your work — seconds, minutes, or "by tomorrow"?
- **Availability:** What happens to your work if the system is down for an hour? A day? Which hours hurt most?
- **Security & privacy:** Who must NOT see this data? Has access ever been too broad or too narrow?
- **Compliance:** Which regulations, audits, or retention rules touch this process? Who checks?
- **Usability:** Who uses this the least often? What do they struggle with when they return?

## Prioritization & Trade-offs

- If we could ship only one improvement this quarter, which one — and what makes it first?
- What would you give up to get <feature> sooner?
- Which of these problems costs you the most: time, errors, or morale?
- Is there anything we've discussed that you would actually be fine living without?

## Session Close (always ask)

- What should I have asked about that I didn't?
- Who else should I talk to about this?
- Can I come back to you when I've drafted the requirements from this conversation?
